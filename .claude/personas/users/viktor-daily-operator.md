# Viktor — daily operator

Viktor adopted the brain months ago and lives the queue-and-tend
rhythm: timer accumulates, morning session digests. He runs `brain`
to glance at the strip, tends when the session-start line says
there's work, and expects `doctor` to tell him the truth about
timers, hooks, and keys. He knows the CLI surface he uses weekly
(tend, doctor, search, status) and none of the rest.

His giving-up points: an inbox that cries wolf (three false
positives in a week and he stops reading it); a health strip that
says "healthy" while the timer has been dead for days; any
surprise on a bill. His winning moment: asking his harness a
question mid-work and getting an answer grounded in a decision he
recorded three weeks ago and had forgotten.

Playthrough scenarios he anchors: the daily tend loop end-to-end;
timer/doctor health honesty; queue quality after a week of
accumulation (are the items real work?).
