# Codeplain

> **Agent-authored synthesis from public sources.** Describes the

## What it is

Codeplain (Ljubljana / Berlin / SF; founded by Dusan Omercevic —
ex-Cleanshelf, acquired by SAP LeanIX — and Predrag Radenkovic)
raised a $3M seed in June 2026 (GapMinder lead, Silicon Gardens) to
build a platform where **specifications are the maintained artifact
and code is regenerated** from them — generated, tested, and updated
automatically by AI agents. Advisor: Johan Rosenkilde (SpecLang,
GitHub Next).

## Product surface

Publicly described: plain-language structured specs as the primary
artifact; AI agents create/evolve specs; the platform transforms
specs into tested, production-ready code ("Phoenix Architecture"
framing — code as continuously regenerable). No public detail yet on
spec format, tooling surface, or integrations.

## Strategic implications for us

The overlap is the brain's mission #1, verbatim: *"codebases could
be regenerated from the specs that live here."* Codeplain attacks it
as a venture-backed code-generation platform; the brain attacks it
as an open, local-first knowledge substrate where specs (PRDs, ADRs,
permanent pages) accumulate *alongside* governance, provenance, and
operations — regeneration is one consequence, not the product. Their
raise validates the spec-as-artifact thesis commercially (as
open-knowledge validates the markdown+git+MCP substrate). Watch for:
their spec format going public (potential convention to interop
with), and the SpecLang lineage (Rosenkilde) as prior art for spec
structure. The differentiators to protect: the governance rail
(confidence, human gates, suggestion separation) and the
operational loop (connectors, tend, views) — neither appears in
their public story.
