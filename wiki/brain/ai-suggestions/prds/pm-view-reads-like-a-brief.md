# Role views read like briefs — reader-voiced headers, human titles, explanatory empty states

> **AI-suggested PRD.** Does **not** reflect a human-approved
> initiative and does **not** record committed work or upcoming
> product changes. This page is **agent-authored synthesis** —
> a *suggestion* a human can review, iterate on, and either
> graduate (drop the `ai_suggestion: true` flag, change status
> to `living`, move file from `wiki/brain/ai-suggestions/prds/`
> to `wiki/brain/prds/`) or supersede with a different framing.
>
> Treat the personas / problem / appetite / metrics below as
> **the agent's hypothesis**, not the team's commitment. The
> PM (human) owns whether this becomes a real initiative.

## Why the agent suggests this

The Priya (non-terminal PM) playthrough of 2026-07-12 fetched
`/_views/custom/pm/` — the view whose declared audience is
"team/pm" — and read it in persona. The first sentence a PM reads
is "Auto-generated by `brain.py views` from `views/pm.yml` — edit
the spec, not this page": a maintenance instruction addressed to
spec editors, on a reading surface. The one populated table keys
its row on a raw file path (`insights/quickstart-third-command-
fragility.md`) before the title; three of four sections render as
"(no rows)" with no hint of what fills them; and "Ingest queue" is
pipeline vocabulary. The persona's in-character verdict, recorded
in the transcript: she would have closed the tab and concluded the
view belongs to whoever owns the YAML. This is a synthetic-walk
inference — no human PM has reported bouncing off the view.

## Inferred objective

If role views led with a reader-voiced framing line (what this
view shows and for whom), linked rows by human title, and replaced
"(no rows)" with one sentence about what would appear there, the
view would function as the meeting brief the PM persona's winning
moment describes — instead of reading as tooling exhaust.

## Affected personas (agent-inferred)

- [Priya — non-terminal PM](../../../../.claude/personas/users/priya-non-terminal-pm.md) —
  the view is nominally hers; her stated winning moment is "the PM
  view giving her the meeting brief she used to spend an hour
  assembling", and her frustration is jargon on reading surfaces.
  The human reviewer should validate this reading.

## Now / Perceived / Target (agent's read)

- **Now** — `views/pm.yml` compiles to a page whose header block,
  row keys, and empty states are provenance-first and path-first
  (observed 2026-07-12 against build 0.19.3; excerpts in the
  transcript). The content itself — initiatives, pitches,
  insights, queue — is the right selection for the audience.
- **Perceived** — the composable-views trail describes the specs
  as role-fit views for engineer/pm/operator audiences; the brain
  appears to believe the audience fit is achieved by row
  *selection*. The playthrough suggests *presentation* is the
  unfinished half.
- **Target (hypothesis)** — each role view opens in its reader's
  language, and a non-terminal reader can use it without knowing
  the view is generated or how to regenerate it.

## Scope (suggested)

The rendered header of custom views: keep the provenance line for
maintainers but demote it (footer or aside), lead with the
audience-facing description the spec already carries. Row
rendering: title first, path secondary or omitted. Empty states:
one generated sentence per section naming what would fill it
(e.g. that open initiatives appear once a pitch is bet on).
Applies to all shipped role views, not just pm.

## No-gos (suggested)

No change to the view-spec YAML contract or the SQL compilation;
no per-view hand-authored prose that would drift from the spec;
no removal of the provenance information (maintainers still need
it — it moves, it doesn't vanish).

## Rabbit holes (suggested)

Auto-generating good empty-state sentences for arbitrary operator-
defined specs could balloon; shipping them only for the bundled
role views with a generic fallback keeps the appetite small.
Redesigning the views system's rendering pipeline wholesale is out
of proportion to the observed friction.

## Appetite (estimated)

Small — the change is in how the views renderer emits headers,
row links, and empty sections. Load-bearing only on the
technical-complexity dimension.

## Suggested success metrics

A re-run of the Priya scenario reads the PM view without meeting
maintainer-addressed language above the fold, path-keyed rows, or
unexplained "(no rows)"; the in-character give-up verdict on the
view flips from "would not return" to usable-as-brief.

## Open questions for the human reviewer

- Is the affected persona real and the framing accurate?
- Does this overlap with in-flight work on the views system the
  agent doesn't know about?
- Is the appetite plausible given current team capacity?
- Should empty-state sentences live in the view spec (per-block
  description fields) rather than the renderer?
- Is the suggestion landing in the right scope (brain-meta)?

## Suggested next step

- **Graduate** if the reviewer agrees the role views' presentation
  should match their declared audiences. (Drop
  `ai_suggestion: true`, status to `living`, move to
  `wiki/brain/prds/`, hand to Tech Lead.)
- **Iterate** if the fix belongs in the spec format rather than
  the renderer.
- **Reject** if role views are intentionally maintainer-first and
  the PM-facing brief is meant to be the briefing page alone.
