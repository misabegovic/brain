`ui/.build-cache` from the primary checkout, verified via the

## Step 4 — search

Expectation: search finds a decision she knows exists.

The search UI is client-side Pagefind; this harness could not
drive the JS input (no interactive browser available to the
agent), so the probe went one layer down: fetch the *served*
index and query it. First attempt — against `ui/dist` in the
primary checkout — showed 6 of 22 ADRs missing, including
persona-playthrough-loop: a false alarm. The server actually
serves `ui/.build-cache`, whose index is complete:

    fragments: 71
    ADRs on disk: 22 | indexed: 22
    playthrough content hit: True

Recorded both because a real user reporting "search can't find
X" could equally be looking at a stale parallel build; the
product surface itself was healthy. Superseded ADRs are indexed
too (they carry the banner when opened), which is the right call
for trail questions. Interactive typing-and-results behaviour
remains unverified in this walk — index-level verification only.

## Step 5 — the AI-draft vs approved distinction (her trust scenario)

Expectation: she can always tell an AI draft from an approved
decision.

What the corpus offered: **zero** pages under any
`ai-suggestions/` shelf. The aggregated view
(`/_views/ai-suggestions/`, real fetch) renders and says "0
suggestion(s)". The onboarding deck (real fetch — zero
terminal-command lines in the whole deck, to its credit) tells
her: "A page in ai-suggestions/ is NOT a decision the team has
made. The UI renders the distinction on every card and page —
trust the banner."

In character: "You're asking me to trust a banner I have never
seen, and nobody can show me one, because none exist." The
v0.17.0 Priya walk recorded the same and deferred to "the first
real suggestion". Two versions later it still had never rendered.

This sweep closed the loop for real: it authored the first live
suggestion (`wiki/brain/ai-suggestions/prds/priya-trust-banner-render-proof.md`,
this walk's routed finding), built the UI against the worktree
wiki, and inspected the output:

    [page chrome] initiative · draft · confidence: low ·
      appetite: small
    AI suggestion. Agent-authored draft — not a human-approved
    decision, not current or upcoming product state. Review,
    iterate, and graduate (or discard) it.
    [briefing card, Needs-you band] AI draft — review to graduate

The banner and the briefing labelling both work — verified
rendering for the first time against live content. The finding
stands regardless: the proof existed only because this walk
manufactured content to exercise it, and it decays back to
unproven the moment the draft graduates or is discarded. (The
build also caught a YAML-unsafe `summary:` in the draft's own
frontmatter — a colon inside an unquoted scalar broke the content
sync; fixed with a block scalar. Noted as authoring-side
friction, not a reader-surface defect.)

  decision-worthy; routed as the walk's one AI-suggestion:

- PM view speaks to maintainers, surfaces raw paths/tiers, no

  one-suggestion budget this walk carries; recorded here for a
  future walk or operator judgement.
- Stray "send" label on card actions — transcript-only.
- Stale parallel `ui/dist` index (false-alarm source) —
  transcript-only; the served surface was correct.
- Interactive search UI and `/api/act` channel — not executable
  from this harness/protocol; recorded as coverage gaps, not
  defects.
